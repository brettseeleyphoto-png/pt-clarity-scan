Why this exists

I have worked with a lot of trainers. Some of the best coaches I know are also some of the worst marketers I have ever seen — and it is not because they do not care. It is because nobody taught them to talk about their work the way their work actually deserves to be talked about.

Fitness changed my life. I know what it means to be on the other side of a great coach — someone who noticed things about you before you noticed them yourself, who held a standard you didn't know you needed, who changed the way you thought about what you were capable of.

Most of the trainers I have worked with have that same quality. They just have no idea how to show it to the people who need it most.

"Having the story is one thing.
Being able to deliver it is the other."

The PT Clarity Scan finds the story. What you do with it after is the work. This tool is my way of helping coaches I respect build something that matches how good they actually are.

The problem in four words

Try explaining your coaching without using these.

Results
Accountability
Transformation
Lifestyle

Every trainer uses all four. They stopped meaning anything the moment everyone started using them.

The question is not whether you get results. The question is: what do you actually do differently that the right client would care about — and can you say it in a way no other trainer could?

What happens inside

Five phases. One real conversation.

45 to 75 minutes. It will push back on vague answers. Specific moments beat polished stories.

01
Warm Up
Your current story — how you talk about your work right now, and where the generic language is hiding.
02
First 100 Lunges
The moment that shaped how you coach. The lie you had to unlearn. What fitness changed that nobody could see from the outside.
03
Ass-to-Ankle Squats
The client you will never forget. The client you should have said no to earlier. What your best clients have in common — beyond wanting results.
04
Burnout Set
What you believe that half the industry would push back on. The conversations you should own. What you are willing to say plainly that other trainers soften.
05
Final Rep
Confirm the strongest material. Identify what you are still tempted to polish. Name the one reason the right client should trust you.
What you leave with: An 11-section profile that explains how you see fitness, what you stand for, why you can guide this, and who is actually built for your coaching — plus a content direction and a lead filter so you stop wasting time on clients who were never going to stay.
